Visualization 1 · Overview

Monthly Sales and Profit Over Time (2014–2017)

Sales and profit both grow year-over-year, with clear Q4 seasonality every year. However, profit consistently lags behind sales — suggesting that revenue growth alone does not guarantee profitability, and that discount-driven sales may be eroding margins.

Visualization 2 · Key Relationship

Discount Rate vs. Profit by Category

There is a clear negative relationship between discount rate and profit at the order-line level. Orders with discounts above 20% frequently fall below the break-even line, and Furniture shows the highest concentration of deeply discounted, loss-making orders. Click a category in the legend to highlight it.

Visualization 3 · Category & Region

Net Profit by State

Profit varies significantly across states — California and New York lead, while Texas, Ohio, and Pennsylvania show heavy losses. Use the Category dropdown to filter by product category, then drill into a specific Sub-Category to see how performance shifts geographically.

Visualization 4 · Subgroup Focus

Does Ship Mode Affect Profit at the Same Price Point?

Across all three categories, Standard Class shipping combined with heavy discounts produces the worst average profit — shown in deep red. Furniture sub-categories like Tables and Furnishings are consistently loss-making regardless of ship mode, while Technology sub-categories remain profitable even under Standard Class.

Visualization 5 · D3 Deep Dive

Why Do Furniture Tables & Bookcases Lose Money?

This Sankey diagram traces how orders flow from ship mode through discount level to their final outcome (profit or loss). Standard Class with discounts above 20% is the dominant loss pathway. Use the Category dropdown to compare categories, and drag the discount slider to see how losses accumulate as higher discounts are included. Click any node to focus on its connected flows.
