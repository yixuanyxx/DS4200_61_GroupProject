Visualization 1 · Overview

Monthly Sales and Profit Over Time (2014–2017)

Sales and profit both grow year-over-year, with clear Q4 seasonality every year. However, profit consistently lags behind sales — suggesting that revenue growth alone does not guarantee profitability, and that discount-driven sales may be eroding margins.

Visualization 2 · Key Relationship

Discount Rate vs. Profit by Category

There is a clear negative relationship between discount rate and profit at the order-line level. Orders with discounts above 20% frequently fall below the break-even line, and Furniture shows the highest concentration of deeply discounted, loss-making orders. Click a category in the legend to highlight it.

Visualization 3 · Category & Region

Profit by Sub-Category and Region

Technology sub-categories dominate profit, while Tables and Bookcases in the Furniture category are structural loss-makers across all regions — especially Central and East. Use the dropdown to isolate a specific region and compare performance within it.

Visualization 4 · Subgroup Focus

Furniture: Sales vs. Profit by Sub-Category and Region

Tables stand out as the clearest problem case: they generate substantial sales volume across all regions, yet consistently produce negative profit. Bookcases show a similar pattern on a smaller scale. This disconnect between sales and profit points directly to excessive discounting as the primary driver of losses.

Visualization 5 · D3 Deep Dive

Why Do Furniture Tables & Bookcases Lose Money?

Use the dropdowns to filter by region or sub-category, then brush the scatter plot to select a discount range. The bar chart on the right updates instantly to show how profit breaks down by shipping mode within that selection — revealing that Standard Class orders with high discounts account for the bulk of losses.
